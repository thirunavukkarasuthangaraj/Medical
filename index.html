FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Homeopathy?

Homeopathy is a natural form of medicine that has been used for over 200 years. It works on the principle of "like cures like" - a substance that causes symptoms in a healthy person can cure similar symptoms in a sick person when given in very small doses. It's safe, gentle, and effective for all ages.

Is homeopathy safe for children and pregnant women?

Yes, homeopathy is completely safe for children, pregnant women, and nursing mothers. The medicines are highly diluted natural substances with no toxic side effects. Many parents prefer homeopathy for their children because of its gentle action and safety profile.

How long does homeopathic treatment take?

The duration of treatment depends on the condition being treated. Acute conditions like cold, fever, or minor injuries may resolve in hours to days. Chronic conditions that have developed over years may take several months to a year for complete healing. Dr. Devini will provide an estimated timeline during your consultation.

Can I take homeopathy along with allopathic medicine?

Yes, in most cases homeopathic medicines can be taken alongside allopathic medicines without any interaction. However, it's important to inform Dr. Devini about all medications you're currently taking so she can plan your treatment accordingly and guide you properly.

What should I expect during my first consultation?

Your first consultation will typically last 30-45 minutes. Dr. Devini will take a detailed case history including your current symptoms, medical history, lifestyle, dietary habits, sleep patterns, and mental-emotional state. This comprehensive approach helps in selecting the most suitable remedy for your individual constitution.

Are there any dietary restrictions during treatment?

Generally, it's advised to avoid strong-smelling substances like coffee, raw onion, garlic, and strong mints around the time of taking medicines as they may interfere with the remedy. Dr. Devini will provide specific dietary guidelines based on your condition and the medicines prescribed.
